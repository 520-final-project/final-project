Our oceans and consumption patterns are at a tipping point. Here's what's happening — and the concrete steps each of us can take.

The Problems 🔴

Decades of unsustainable production, overconsumption, and neglect have pushed our oceans to their limits. Understanding these problems is the first step.

🧴

Plastic Pollution

Over 8 million metric tons of plastic enter our oceans each year. Microplastics have infiltrated every corner of the ocean — from surface waters to the deepest trenches — and now appear in the seafood we eat and the water we drink.

📊 11M tonnes/year entering oceans
🌡️

Ocean Warming & Acidification

The ocean has absorbed over 90% of the excess heat from climate change and about 30% of all human CO₂ emissions. This causes mass coral bleaching events, rising sea levels, and the acidification of seawater — making it harder for shellfish and coral to form shells.

🌡️ +0.13°C per decade since 1901
🎣

Overfishing

More than 34% of the world's fish stocks are exploited at biologically unsustainable levels. Industrial fishing fleets use destructive methods — bottom trawling, dynamite fishing — that obliterate entire coral reef ecosystems and seagrass beds in minutes.

🎣 34% of stocks overfished
🏭

Overconsumption & Waste

Global material consumption tripled between 1970 and 2017 and is projected to double again by 2060. Our throwaway culture generates billions of tonnes of waste annually — much of which ends up polluting land and water systems worldwide.

🗑️ 2.1B tonnes of solid waste/year
⚗️

Chemical & Nutrient Pollution

Agricultural fertilisers, industrial discharge, and untreated sewage wash vast quantities of nitrogen and phosphorus into coastal waters. This triggers toxic algal blooms and creates massive "dead zones" — oxygen-depleted areas where almost no marine life can survive.

💀 700+ dead zones worldwide
🪸

Coral Reef Collapse

Coral reefs support an estimated 25% of all marine species yet cover less than 1% of the ocean floor. Half of the world's shallow-water coral reefs have been lost in the last 30 years due to bleaching, ocean acidification, and physical destruction.

🪸 50% of reefs lost since 1950

The Visual Truth 📸

The difference between a healthy ocean and a polluted one is stark — and the choice of which future we leave behind is ours to make.

✅ A Healthy Ocean
Crystal-clear waters teeming with biodiversity. Coral reefs alive with colour. Fish stocks thriving and feeding billions. This is what we're fighting to protect.
❌ A Polluted Ocean
Choked with waste, dead zones spreading, coral bleached and dying. Marine life suffocating in plastic. This is the reality if we don't act now.
What We Can Do 🌱

Every individual action adds up. Here are the most impactful ways you can contribute to healthier oceans and more sustainable consumption.

♻️

Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Eliminating single-use plastics from your daily routine is one of the highest-impact changes you can make. Choose reusable bags, bottles, straws, and containers. Support brands with verified sustainable packaging and closed-loop design.

💡 Tip: Carry a reusable cup — saving just one disposable cup per day means 365 fewer cups in landfills per year.
🛒

Consume Responsibly

Buy only what you need and choose sustainably sourced seafood certified by the Marine Stewardship Council (MSC) or Aquaculture Stewardship Council (ASC). Support circular economy businesses that design waste out of their products from the start.

💡 Tip: Check the Seafood Watch app before buying fish to see which choices are ocean-friendly.
🏖️

Join Clean-Up Initiatives

Participate in local beach, river, or neighbourhood clean-up events. A single volunteer session can remove hundreds of pieces of litter before they reach the ocean. Organisations like Ocean Conservancy run global clean-up days you can join.

💡 Tip: Log your finds during clean-ups — data on what litter is most common helps drive policy change.
📢

Advocate & Educate

Share what you learn with family, friends, and your community. Support policies that restrict single-use plastics, strengthen marine protected areas, and enforce fishing regulations. Write to representatives and sign evidence-based petitions.

💡 Tip: Sharing a single accurate fact about ocean pollution on social media reaches hundreds of people instantly.
🌿

Shift to Sustainable Habits

Reducing food waste, composting organic material, and eating more plant-rich meals all lower agricultural demand — cutting the runoff that creates ocean dead zones. Even small dietary shifts across billions of people have a measurable planetary impact.

💡 Tip: Reducing meat consumption by one meal per day can cut your food-related carbon footprint by up to 50%.
💰

Vote with Your Wallet

Every purchase is a vote for the kind of world you want. Choose companies that are transparent about their environmental impact, use renewable energy, minimise packaging, and actively contribute to ocean conservation efforts.

💡 Tip: Look for B Corp certification or third-party sustainability ratings when choosing brands.
